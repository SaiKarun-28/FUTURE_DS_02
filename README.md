## üîé Dashboard Features & Visualizations

### 1. Campaign Performance Analysis
* **Revenue by Campaign:** A pie chart visualization showing the dominance of the **"Festive Offer Drive"** (60.97% of revenue), followed by the "Fashion Week Special" (31.93%).
* **Engagement Funnel:** A funnel chart highlighting that the "Festive Offer Drive" also leads in engagement rate (800.00), significantly outperforming the "Summer Buzz Campaign."
* **Temporal Trends:** An area chart tracking engagement rates over time, highlighting specific peaks in late August (likely correlating with campaign launch dates).

### 2. Demographic Insights
* **Age Analysis:**
    * **Clicks by Age:** The **30-34 age group** generates the highest volume of clicks across all campaigns.
    * **Engagement by Age:** Engagement drops sharply after the 30-34 age bracket, indicating this is the primary target audience.
    * **Revenue by Age:** The 30-34 demographic contributes **42.97%** of the total revenue.
* **Gender Distribution:** A clustered bar chart comparing revenue contributions by gender across different campaigns, showing a relatively balanced split with a slight female lead in the "Festive Offer Drive."

## üí° Key Business Insights derived from Data

1.  **Top Performer:** The **Festive Offer Drive** is the undisputed champion of this cycle, generating the majority of both revenue and engagement.
2.  **Target Audience:** The data strongly suggests that the ideal customer profile is within the **30-34 age range**. Marketing efforts targeting users above 40 yield significantly lower ROI and engagement.
3.  **Campaign Lag:** The **Summer Buzz Campaign** is underperforming (only 7.1% of revenue). A strategic review is recommended to either optimize the creative assets or reallocate its budget to the "Fashion Week" or "Festive" categories.
4.  **Conversion Funnel:** While impressions are high (79M), the total conversion count is 2K. This suggests a need to optimize the **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** and landing page experience to close the gap between views and purchases.
